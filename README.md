<h1>Why games were created</h1> 
<p>Computer games were created to entertain and challenge players. They originated as simple text-based games in the mid-20th century, serving as a form of interactive storytelling and intellectual amusement. 
Over time, advances in technology led to the development of more sophisticated graphics and gameplay mechanics, enabling immersive experiences. 
Games also serve educational purposes, teaching problem-solving, strategy, and creativity. 
They provide a platform for social interaction and competition, fostering communities of gamers worldwide. Additionally, the gaming industry has become a significant economic force, generating billions in revenue and driving innovation in hardware and software technologies.</p>
